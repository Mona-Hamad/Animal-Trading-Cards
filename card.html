Beluga Whale

Belugas are the smallest of all whales and they look like large dolphins.

  • Scientific Name: Delphinapterus leucas
  • Average Length: 13 to 20 feet
  • Average Lifespan: 50 years
  • Habitat: in coastal waters of Arctic Ocean and in the subarctic regions

The beluga whale is white as an adult and molts seasonally. The beluga's body is stout and has a small, blunt head with a beak, a well-defined neck, and a rounded melon. It has no dorsal fin, which makes swimming under Arctic ice sheets easier.
